## Offer Diagnosis

Evaluate the offer across six questions:

1. Is the market hungry?
2. Is the avatar specific?
3. Is the dream outcome obvious?
4. Is the likelihood of success believable?
5. Is the time to first win short?
6. Is the effort/sacrifice low enough for the price?

If any answer is weak, improve that part before writing conversion copy.

## Grand Slam Offer Builder

Use this sequence:

1. Avatar: define the narrow buyer.
2. Pain: name the expensive or urgent problem.
3. Dream outcome: state the result in buyer language.
4. Mechanism: explain the method, system, or process that makes the result believable.
5. Delivery: list what happens and how.
6. Quick win: identify the first visible progress point.
7. Proof: add evidence or placeholders.
8. Bonuses: remove objections.
9. Guarantee: reverse the real risk.
10. Scarcity/urgency: explain why action is time-sensitive.
11. CTA: give one clear next step.

Core formula:

```text
We help [avatar] achieve [specific result] in [timeframe/context] without [common sacrifice], using [mechanism], backed by [risk reversal].
```

## Value Equation Prompts

Dream outcome:

- What do they secretly wish would happen?
- What result would make the purchase feel obvious?
- What is the emotional payoff beyond the practical result?

Perceived likelihood:

- What proof would someone like them trust?
- What mechanism explains why this works where alternatives failed?
- What diagnosis shows the seller understands the problem?

Time delay:

- What can happen in the first 24-48 hours?
- What is the first milestone?
- What setup removes uncertainty quickly?

Effort and sacrifice:

- What can be done for them?
- What can be templated?
- What can be automated?
- What can be removed from the process?

## Pricing and Packaging

Use premium pricing when the offer has enough value, proof, and delivery confidence. Explain price through:

- Cost of inaction.
- Economic upside.
- Emotional relief.
- Time saved.
- Risk reduced.
- Status or certainty gained.

Do not discount by default. If price resistance appears:

- Re-anchor value.
- Add relevant bonuses.
- Offer payment terms.
- Reduce scope for a lower tier.

## Bonus Stack

Every bonus should remove an objection or accelerate success.

Bonus template:

```text
Bonus name:
Objection removed:
What it does:
Why it matters:
Value anchor:
Scarcity/urgency if real:
```

Strong bonus types:

- Checklists.
- Templates.
- Scripts.
- Audits.
- Swipe files.
- Implementation sessions.
- Calculators.
- Partner discounts.
- Onboarding support.
- Training for team members.

## Guarantees

Guarantees should reverse the buyer's real risk without creating reckless business risk.

Types:

- Unconditional refund.
- Conditional guarantee based on required action.
- Implementation guarantee.
- Response-time guarantee.
- Performance-based model.
- Anti-guarantee that filters poor-fit buyers.

Guarantee template:

```text
If [buyer does required actions] and does not achieve [defined milestone], we will [risk reversal].
```

Avoid guarantees when proof is weak, delivery is unvalidated, fraud risk is high, or claims are regulated.

## Scarcity and Urgency

Use only real reasons:

- Limited implementation capacity.
- Cohort start date.
- Deadline for bonuses.
- Price increase.
- Seasonal event.
- Limited audit slots.
- Partner promotion window.

CTA formula:

```text
Take [action] by [deadline/capacity condition] so you can [desired next step/result].
```

## Offer Naming

Name the offer to attract the right avatar and imply the result.

Name ingredients:

- Avatar.
- Desired result.
- Timeframe.
- Mechanism.
- Constraint removed.
- Status.

Patterns:

```text
[Result] Sprint
[Avatar] Growth System
[Timeframe] [Outcome] Challenge
The [Mechanism] Method
[Constraint-Free] [Outcome] Program
```

Generate at least 10 names, then select 3 to test in copy.

## Deliverables

For an offer request, output:

- One-line offer.
- Avatar.
- Pain and cost of inaction.
- Dream outcome.
- Unique mechanism.
- What is included.
- Bonus stack.
- Guarantee.
- Scarcity/urgency.
- Price framing.
- CTA.
- 3-10 names.
- Risks or assumptions.
