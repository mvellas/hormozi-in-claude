## Core Four

Every acquisition plan should map to:

- Warm outreach: one-to-one to known contacts.
- Free content: one-to-many to a warm or warming audience.
- Cold outreach: one-to-one to strangers.
- Paid ads: one-to-many to cold audiences.

Scale with:

- More volume.
- Better message/offer/proof.
- New channels, formats, audiences, or angles.

## Lead Magnets

A good lead magnet solves a narrow problem and reveals the next problem solved by the core offer.

Types:

- Diagnosis.
- Sample/trial.
- One process step.
- Template/tool.
- Calculator.
- Mini-training.
- Audit.

Lead magnet output:

```text
Name:
Avatar:
Narrow problem:
Promise:
Format:
Time to consume:
Next problem revealed:
CTA:
Follow-up sequence:
```

## Paid Ads

Ad structure:

1. Avatar callout.
2. Pain or desired result.
3. Mechanism or proof.
4. Offer/lead magnet.
5. CTA.
6. Reason to act now.

Create variants by changing:

- Avatar.
- Pain.
- Outcome.
- Mechanism.
- Proof.
- CTA.
- Creative concept.
- Funnel step.

Do not invent results. Use proof placeholders if missing.

## Outbound

Cold message:

```text
Hi [name], noticed [specific observation].

Usually [avatar] dealing with this is trying to [desired result] but gets stuck because [problem].

I help [avatar] get [result] without [sacrifice] using [mechanism].

Want me to send over [low-friction asset/diagnosis]?
```

Follow-up sequence:

1. Add a specific insight.
2. Share a relevant proof point.
3. Ask a binary question.
4. Offer a low-friction resource.
5. Breakup email.

Warm message:

```text
I am testing a new way to help [avatar] [result]. Since you know [context], would you be open to giving feedback or pointing me to someone dealing with [pain]?
```

## Content

Content unit:

- Hook attention.
- Hold attention.
- Reward attention.
- CTA to next step.

Story structure:

1. Situation.
2. Tension.
3. Attempt.
4. Insight.
5. Result.
6. Lesson.
7. CTA.

Create content from:

- Objections.
- Sales calls.
- Customer wins.
- Mistakes.
- Behind the scenes.
- Myths.
- Before/after.
- Process breakdowns.

## Affiliates and Partners

Partner offer must answer:

- What does the partner's audience get?
- What does the partner get?
- How much work is required?
- How does tracking/payment work?
- What assets are provided?
- Why will it protect or improve trust with their audience?

Partner models:

- Partner gives away your lead magnet.
- Partner sells your lead magnet.
- Partner sells your core offer.
- Joint webinar or launch.
- Bundle or bonus integration.

## Metrics

Track:

- Reach/impressions.
- Clicks.
- Leads.
- Bookings.
- Show rate.
- Close rate.
- CAC.
- Payback.
- LTV.
- Referral rate.

Tie every copy recommendation to the metric it is meant to improve.
