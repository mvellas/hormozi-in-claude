## Sequence Selection

Choose based on the user's goal:

- Nurture: build trust before a sales ask.
- Launch: drive action around a deadline/event.
- Sales follow-up: convert calls, applications, abandoned checkouts, or lead magnet opt-ins.
- Onboarding: create first win and reduce regret.
- Referral: ask after a value moment.
- Winback: reactivate old leads or customers.

## Email Frameworks

Problem-agitation-solution:

```text
Subject: [pain/result]
Open with a specific pain.
Show the cost of ignoring it.
Introduce the mechanism or offer.
CTA.
```

Story-lesson-CTA:

```text
Subject: [curiosity from story]
Tell a concise story.
Extract the lesson.
Connect to the offer.
CTA.
```

Objection email:

```text
Subject: Still wondering if [objection]?
Name the objection.
Validate it.
Reframe with proof/process/guarantee.
CTA.
```

## Launch Sequence

Use whisper/announce/shout:

1. Whisper: tease the problem and upcoming resource.
2. Announce: reveal offer/lead magnet and who it is for.
3. Educate: explain the mechanism.
4. Proof: show case, data, demo, or story.
5. Objection: handle the main concern.
6. Bonus: reveal value stack.
7. Urgency: deadline or capacity reason.
8. Last call: final CTA.

## Nurture Sequence

Five-email default:

1. Welcome and quick win.
2. Problem reframe.
3. Story with lesson.
4. Proof or case breakdown.
5. Invitation to next step.

## Sales Follow-Up

After a call:

1. Recap pain, desired result, and recommended plan.
2. Send proof relevant to their objection.
3. Clarify guarantee/risk reversal.
4. Deadline/capacity reminder if real.
5. Final decision email.

After lead magnet:

1. Deliver asset and explain first action.
2. Explain the next problem.
3. Show the mechanism.
4. Offer diagnosis/call/core offer.

## Onboarding and Referral Emails

Onboarding:

- Confirm purchase.
- Explain what happens next.
- Give one immediate action.
- Set expectations for the first win.

Referral:

- Ask after a result.
- Make the ask specific.
- Provide forwardable text.
- Offer a benefit for the referred person.

## Quality Checklist

- One idea per email.
- One primary CTA.
- Subject line matches the body.
- Specific avatar language.
- Proof is honest.
- Deadline or scarcity is real.
- Email earns the next click.
